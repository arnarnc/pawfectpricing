How to Use

A quick guide to getting the most out of this tool.

🔍

Search eBay

Type a card name or product into the search box, then tap one of the three buttons to open eBay results instantly.

1

Global Sold — worldwide sold listings to see what cards have actually sold for.

2

Australia Sold — filters to Australian sold listings only for local pricing.

3

Cheapest — shows the cheapest active Buy It Now listings globally.

Toggle No auctions (top-right of this section) to filter sold results to Buy It Now only — useful for excluding auction outliers.
✨

Autocomplete

Start typing a Pokémon name and a dropdown will suggest matches. Tap a suggestion to fill the search box — it covers all Pokémon and popular trainer names.

⚡

Shortcuts

Type these short codes anywhere in your search and they expand automatically.

etb→Elite Trainer Box
bbx→Booster Box
nm→Near Mint
lp→Lightly Played
mp→Moderately Played
hp→Heavily Played
dmg→Damaged
Tip: Type Charizard 4 102 nm and it becomes a smart eBay query automatically.
🏆

Graded Cards

Include a grading label and score in your search and the tool automatically switches to Graded Mode, filtering eBay to slabbed cards only.

Examples: PSA 10 · BGS 9.5 · CGC 9 · TAG 10
📋

Comp List

Build a list of cards with prices to calculate a total buy offer for a collection.

1

Type the card name in the search box, enter a $ price, then tap + Add to List.

2

Each row shows the card name and an editable price you can adjust at any time.

3

Tap × to remove a single row, or Clear All to reset the list.

Auto-saved: Your comp list is stored in the browser and survives page refreshes. It only clears when you delete rows.
💸

Payout Percentage

The % column lets you apply a buy percentage to each card. Use the header dropdown to set the same percentage across every row at once.

1

Market Value — total of all raw prices with no discount applied.

2

Payout Offer — total after each card's percentage is applied. This is your buy offer.

Example: $100 @ 80% + $10 @ 70% = $87 payout offer.
